Robert Orzanna

Experimenting With Life’s Fullest Potential

Who am I?

I am a young person seeking to understand what it means to be human. At present I am moving from a convergence1 phase over to divergence, bringing in a better focus into my life.

How do I describe myself in a few keywords?

Degrowth, Sustainable Consumption, Ubuntu, Linux, Open-Source, Guitar, Voluntary Simplicity, Minimalism, Podcasts, Communication, Languages

Why this website?

Primarily this website is meant for myself to help me remember the answers to the questions I keep asking myself repeatedly. If they did answer your questions as well or spark even new ones, then I would love to hear from you by email2.

What do I identify with?

I identify myself through the activities I do regularly. I form part of Sheetgo to help people meet societal challenges through connected spreadsheets3. I approve subtitles for the Amara4 foundation. I am involved in the open-source software movement5. I help Research & Degrowth6 and SCORAI7 with their outreach through online communication. I listen to podcasts8, coordinate9, and record10 them.

What are my life learning lessons?

I guess my main principle in life is to realise my fullest potential every single day. In other words, whatever I do, I want to strive towards doing it the best way I can with my fullest awareness and focus. Reversely, I don’t do things where I feel that I am not acting genuinly or truthfully.

A morning routine is important to iteratively improve a certain condition. Usually I start my day at 05:25 AM to have enough time for myself before starting work at about 09:00 AM. I go through my web bookmark folder that contains a list of daily websites I find important to take a look at. This includes email and several side tasks. I do some exercising and stretching and play the guitar to get into the right mood.

At work, I use a standing desk and use a program to remind myself to take regular breaks from work11. Each time I set, I try to not rely on the leaning of the chair to keep my spine straight and strong. I try not to use my laptop after working hours to avoid new input that re-accelerates my mind instead of calming it down. I go to bed around 10:00 PM to maintain a decent sleep cycle.

And very important — I make the weekends different from my weekly routine.

What am I a little bit proud of?

I cannot help myself but feel a little bit of pride that my co-authored publication “Culture meets Flows: The Cultural Dimension of Urban Metabolism”12 had been cited by the German Advisory Council on Global Change (WBGU) in their report “Humanity on the move: Unlocking the transformative power of cities”13.

What makes me curious at present?

I try to understand and make sense of my own consumption patterns. I guess my goal is to minimise my consumption to a minimum to keep my carbon footprint low and spend my limited time and resources wisely. I want to use them for the things that I truly care about and ignore all the rest. I call this work-in-progress project the “Table of Regular Sustainable Consumption”14.
