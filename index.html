Robert Orzanna

“Life is. It does not require any further concretization because any descriptive attempt would narrow down the incomprehensiveness of what life can all be. How does one live a life that is?”1

Hello and welcome. If you want to reach out to me, please best write me an email2. If you want to know more about me, you can take a look at my CV3.

Currently, my main occupation is to support the sustainable development of Sheetgo4. Besides, I endorse to be lazy. There is nothing wrong about it — and it is sustainable, too.

Here is my current list of “Daily Cool Things To-Do List” items:

  • Begin the day with a morning routine of checking my daily bookmarks folder, drinking coffee and playing guitar.
  • Draw my to-dos as a paper mind-map5.
  • Drink apple cider vinegar.
  • Eat bee pollen.
  • Commute on inline skates.
  • Work at a standing desk.
  • Take micro breaks6.
  • Wake up and go to bed early.
  • Listen to7 and record podcasts8.
  • Improve Ubuntu MATE9.
  • Approve subtitles for the Amara foundation10.
  • Help the FZI11 as a research assistant.
  • Maintain the websites of Research & Degrowth12 and SCORAI13.

Finally, if you are a research scholar, you might be interested in my co-authored publication “Culture meets Flows: The Cultural Dimension of Urban Metabolism”14 that had been cited by the German Advisory Council on Global Change (WBGU) in their report “Humanity on the move: Unlocking the transformative power of cities”15.
